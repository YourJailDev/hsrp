Honolulu Fire Department

The Honolulu Fire Department is a department which is specialized in handling calls related to fire or medical injuries. It consists for 2 divisions. They are the Fire Fighters and the Emergency Medical Service (EMS). Fire Fighters Division are only to handle the fire related calls, for example a house that is on fire, a forest that is on fire, a car/building on fire, etc. Whilst the Emergency Medical Service only handles calls that are medical related, for example a broken ankle, a revival request, etc. Fire Fighters Division consists of Firefighters, while Emergency Medical Service Division consists of Paramedics.

Honolulu Police Department

"Protect and Serve" The Honolulu Police Department is a law enforcement department responsible for the city and it's residents safety. We handle day-to-day issues such as traffic violations, warrant services, and general public protection. While regular officers are restricted to cadet-only gear, trained HPD officers have full access to all non-gamepass gear. We have a specialized team that utilizes extensive training and respond to high risk calls, our Special Weapons and Tactics team is given the very best gear and equipment to handle the worst possible scenarios. The HPD also contains a Detective/Undercover unit tasked and trained with carrying out operations to apprehend our most wanted criminals. Honolulu Sheriff Office

The Honolulu Sheriff's Office is a whitelisted department. You must be an officer in the server to have permission to use liveries from the department. If you are not an officer you may not use liveries from the department. The mission of the Hawaii Sheriffs Office is to provide professional law enforcement services, ensuring the safety and security of the public, and maintaining the integrity and trust of the community we serve.

Department of Homeland Security

The Department of Homeland Security (DHS) is a federal executive department of the United States government responsible for public security, roughly comparable to the interior or home ministries of other countries. Its primary mission is to protect the United States from a wide range of threats and to ensure the safety and security of its citizens. Established in response to the September 11, 2001, terrorist attacks, DHS was created through the Homeland Security Act of 2002 and began operations on March 1, 2003.

Honolulu Department of Transportation

The Hawaii Department of Transportation (HDOT) is responsible for the planning, development, and maintenance of the state's transportation infrastructure, which includes highways, and public transit systems. Established to enhance safe and efficient travel within Hawaii, the DOT focuses on improving transportation solutions while addressing environmental and community needs. It oversees various divisions, such as the Highways Division.
